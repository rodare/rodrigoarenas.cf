Agroecological Cultural Precincts (ACPs), Research period: 2017-ongoing


How to coexist harmoniously with the other, be that person, animal, plant or landscape, so that each can find a space for self-realization through a mutually enriching coexistence? This is an interdisciplinary project that combines Arts and Agroecology to explore the wide range of possibilities to relate to the environment sustainably.



Land Art and Primitive Technology at Ogród Salwatore, 2019-2020


Without any external tools or materials, but exclusively those from the place, three installations were created: "The Tree Indian" (made of clay and leaves), "The long sturdy rope to connect trees" (made of twisted fine fibers), and "The Gym tree" (accomodated to facilitate several calisthenic exercises. Not visible on the pictures).

Acción Ecológica, 2017


Organized a group named: “Ecological Action”, which consisted in a series of walks into beautiful natural areas (specially one that we called “our countryside” to which we went 8 times with a group of about 15 volunteer friends) and we collected the garbage that we found, placed it a city office container (as we requested each time) and after cleaning the area (private or public), we made a picnic on it, played music (usually three guitars, flutes, and percussion) and did different activities concerning the environment and sustainability. Many times children came and through this acts we all reinforced the need to be aware about environmental pollution.




Dialogical Artifacts Project, 2015


Collaborated with this project by filming, and helping in the design and testing of some of the dialogical artifacts. They consist of sculptures and artistic installations that produced sound from human and natural sources of action, such as the wind, water, sun, gravity, etc.

Short films in collaboration with Francisco Huichaqueo, 2011


Produced two short films in collaboration with Francisco Huichaqueo, a Mapuche film-director. The first one titled "El Ronco" explores the moral perspective of a man that has recently been liberated from prison, where he has spent most of his life.

The second one, titled "El Muro" attempts to rediscover the commonly-ignored textures of the walls that surround us.
